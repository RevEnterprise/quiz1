Local Food of My Hometown

Nasi Megono

Nasi Megono (Megono Rice) is a traditional dish that has been a staple in Batang for generations. The main ingredient is young jackfruit (nangka), locally known as cecek. The young jackfruit is chopped and mixed with grated coconut and a variety of spices, creating a rich, savory flavor and aroma. Nasi Megono is typically served with mendoan (a popular fried tempeh) and traditionally wrapped in banana leaves.

Lontong Lamprak

Lontong Lamprak is a popular dish that can be found in almost any places. It is not much different from other types of lontong. The filling is usually chicken opor seasoned with spices such as pepper, turmeric, shallots, chives, brown sugar, bay leaves, and areca nut root, though other ingredients may be added depending on preference. What makes it unique is the way it is enjoyed, the way it usually eaten is while sitting cross-legged on a mat, which is also the origin of its name.

Gethuk Mawur

Gethuk Mawur is a well-known side dish or snack made from cassava (singkong). It is very similar to another dish called gethuk iris, with the main difference between them in the processing method. While gethuk iris is ground until smooth, gethuk mawur is ground into small granules shape. This snack is usually served with grated coconut.
